Dr. Ir. Soekarno

Founding Father of Indonesia

Learn more

1901-1929

  • 1901Born in Surabaya, West Java.
  • 1926Completed Bachelor of Civil Engineering Education at Technische Hoge School, Bandung.
  • 1927Established the Indonesian National Party, a pro-independent party which advocated independence for Indonesia. Soekarno was elected as its first leader.
  • 1929Arrested by the Dutch for political activities in the Indonesian National Party.

1930-1934

  • 1930Compose the speech "Indonesia Menggoegat" while incarcerated. Provides defense for himself incarcerated at Landraad courthouse hearing in Bandung by attacking imperialism and colonialism. Then Soekarno was sentenced four years of prison.
  • 1931Sukarno was released from as a cause of his speech receiving extensive cover by the press and a strong pressure by the liberal elements in both Netherlands and East Dutch Indies.
  • 1932Became head of Partindo party which was formed by the former members of then disbanded Indonesian National Party.
  • 1934Exiled by Netherland authorities to a small town Ende in Flores, Indonesia to prevent Sukarno from making political speeches. He utilised his limited freedom to established a children’s theatre.

1942-1946

  • 1942Regained freedom as a cause of Japanese invasion of Dutch East Indies. Sukarno and other nationalist wanted to use the Japanese to gain independence for Indonesia.
  • 1944Involved in the formation of PETA and Heiho, a volunteer army troops, which by the mid-1945 had around two million units and were preparing to defeat Allied forces in case of an attempt to retake Java.
  • 1945Declared independence of the Republic of Indonesia and was appointed as President of Republic of Indonesia
  • 1946Took part in the negations with the Netherlands to reach an agreement of the state of Indonesia’s Sovereignty, resulted in the internationally brokered agreement the Linggarjati Agreement.

1949-1970

  • 1949After the Netherlands broke their part of the agreement multiple times, it caused an international outrage. In which, Sukarno played a part in the signing of Dutch-Indonesian Round Table Conference, causing the Netherlands to a complete transfer of Sovereignty to Republic Indonesia.
  • 1955Organised The Bandung Conference, aimed to unite the developing African and Asian countries to the Non-Aligned Movement.
  • 1957Nationalised 246 Dutch companies which had been dominating the Indonesian economy.
  • 1960Stabilized the ongoing conflict around Indonesia, whether from domestic forces or non domestic one’s
  • 1970Died of kidney failure in Jakarta Army Hospital at age 69
